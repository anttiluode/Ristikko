One looping nonlinear medium holds an attractor. Its shape carries two independent bits that its loudness cannot reveal: the parity (which way the orbit is oriented) and the arrow (which way it is traversed). A homodyne reference reads the parity and is blind to the arrow; a lag reference reads the arrow and is blind to the parity. Seed a packet, watch the drone, and read both back at once. Store the seed and recall re-locks the orbit — the picture is a consequence, never the payload.

Past toy · noise-robustness benchmark

Corrupt each stored attractor with rising noise, let the medium relax, and count how often each bit comes back. This turns the shape into a measured quantity instead of an admired one.

The registered expectation: parity is a full-amplitude sign (≈2√μ) and rides out heavy noise; the arrow is a small 4ε imbalance and fails first. Where the cyan curve falls is the arrow's resolution limit — Ristikko's ε dial, drawn.

The honest ledger — what this is, what it is not

The medium is real, not decorative. Two counter-rotating Stuart–Landau modes with delayed feedback: ż = (μ+iΩ)z − |z|²z + κ·z(t−τ) + noise, Ω = ±ω. The −|z|²z term is Kaiku's α knee — it caps the radius at √μ and is what makes the orbit an attractor rather than a decaying or exploding transient. The delayed tap is the recurrent cable.

The two readouts are the two halves of the field. [V] Parity = global phase (z→−z), read by the homodyne projection and blind to the arrow because |z_R|+|z_L| is fixed. Arrow = current imbalance |z_R|²−|z_L|² (the chiral/lag current), read by the lag reference and blind to parity because |·|² kills the global sign. This is Ristikko's lattice: two conjugation-odd sectors, two references, no cross-talk — here by construction, so the meters are honest demonstrations, not fits.

Save the seed, not the picture. A slot stores {parity, arrow, μ, ω, κ, τ, ε} and a thumbnail. Recall re-seeds the live medium and it physically re-locks; the meters confirm the bits returned. The thumbnail is the album you asked for, but it is a consequence of the seed, never the stored thing. This is the nonlinear descendant of EDSAC's mercury delay-line memory — the seed is a valid initial condition that lands back in the basin, provided the parameters are restored too (the attractor is the vector field, not the point).

What it is not. This is classical DSP in floating point — a faithful twin of the VB-cable loop, not a more "physical" or quantum thing. A software loopback moves the same float buffers through RAM; its jitter is worse-characterised noise, not realer noise. Genuine physical substrate (thermal entropy, analog relaxation) lives in hardware — an analog circuit, an optical loop, a memristor crossbar — and no amount of cable makes this that. [retired] "topological soliton arithmetic for free" — a Stuart–Landau delay loop is not an integrable soliton system and will not spontaneously do algebra.

Where it goes to stop being a toy. A single nonlinear node with delayed feedback, time-multiplexed, is a delay-based reservoir computer (Appeltant et al., 2011); the parity-locked-to-0/π cell is an Ising spin, and a network of them is a coherent Ising machine. The honest next step is a trained linear readout on a real task — memory capacity, one-step chaotic prediction — measured against a baseline. The benchmark above is the first step onto that path: it reports a number, and the number has a registered expectation.
